Divvy usage by time-of-day¶

authors: Peter Carbonetto and Gao Wang
date: July 12, 2017

Here we use the Divvy trip data to examine biking trends over the course of a typical day in Chicago.

I begin by loading a few packages, as well as some additional functions I wrote for this project. The repr package is used to adjust the dimensions of the figures in the Jupyter notebook.

In [8]:
library(repr)
library(data.table)
library(ggplot2)
source("../code/functions.R")

Converting dates and times.

To make it easier to compile statistics by time of day, I convert the "start hour" column to a factor.

In [3]:
divvy$trips <- transform(divvy$trips,start.hour = factor(start.hour,0:23))

Count departures by time-of-day¶

Now that start.hour is a factor, it is easy to create a bar chart showing the total number of departures at each hour. Unsurprisingly, we se very little biking activity at night. Further, the two peaks ("modes") in the bar chart nicely recapitulate the morning and afternoon rush hours.

In [4]:
options(repr.plot.width = 5, repr.plot.height = 2.5)
ggplot(divvy$trips,aes(start.hour)) +
  geom_bar(fill = "dodgerblue",width = 0.6) +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(panel.grid.major = element_blank(),
        panel.grid.minor = element_blank())

However, this bar chart is a bit muddled because it is counting trips during the week and on the weekends. Once we separate the counts by the day of the week, the rush-hour trends become more obvious. (Also notice that the rush-hour weeks disappear on Saturday and Sunday.)

In [5]:
options(repr.plot.width = 6, repr.plot.height = 6)
ggplot(divvy$trips,aes(start.hour)) +
  geom_bar(fill = "dodgerblue",width = 0.75) +
  facet_wrap(~start.day,ncol = 2) +
  scale_x_discrete(breaks = seq(0,24,2)) +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(panel.grid.major = element_blank(),
        panel.grid.minor = element_blank())

Time-of-day trends at University of Chicago¶

The commuting trends are different at the University of Chicago Divvy station---there isn't much of a morning rush hour. This may be because students and staff don't regularly use the Divvy bikes for commuting.

In [6]:
options(repr.plot.width = 6, repr.plot.height = 6)
ggplot(subset(divvy$trips,from_station_name == "University Ave & 57th St"),
       aes(start.hour)) +
  geom_bar(fill = "dodgerblue",width = 0.75) +
  facet_wrap(~start.day,ncol = 2) +
  scale_x_discrete(breaks = seq(0,24,2)) +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(panel.grid.major = element_blank(),
        panel.grid.minor = element_blank())
